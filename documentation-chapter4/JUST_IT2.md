## ⚠️ Parameters and Choices That Need Justification

### 1. Target ratio: strict 1:1 (50/50)
**Issue:** A 1:1 ratio is the most common default, but it is not universally optimal. The literature on imbalanced learning notes that the optimal resampling ratio depends on the original imbalance severity and the dataset size. For the GPT case, the original ratio is ~2.5:1 (A=1013 vs R=404), which is considered **moderate** imbalance. For moderate imbalance, strict 1:1 is defensible but not the only well-supported choice — ratios like 2:1 or 3:2 sometimes preserve more majority-class information while still mitigating bias. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10374143/)

**Your defense:** 1:1 is by far the most widely cited target ratio in the imbalanced learning literature and is the default in tools like `imbalanced-learn`. It is easy to justify by citing He & Garcia (2009, "Learning from Imbalanced Data") or Kotsiantis et al. (2006). State it explicitly as a deliberate design choice and note that finding the optimal ratio is left to future work.

***

### 2. Information loss from 60% majority-class discard
**Issue:** Discarding 609 out of 1,013 A-samples is aggressive. The NLP-specific imbalanced learning survey (EACL 2023)  and a 2025 text classification study  both flag that undersampling in text tasks risks losing **semantically unique** majority-class samples, since each text sample may capture distinct linguistic patterns that are not redundant. This is particularly relevant here because the A-class (no-retrieval) queries are diverse question types, not simple numeric records. [aclanthology](https://aclanthology.org/2023.eacl-main.38.pdf)

**Your defense:** Since the training set is small to begin with (1,417 samples), you can acknowledge this limitation directly and motivate IT3 (class-weighted CE) and IT4 (Focal Loss) as algorithm-level alternatives that avoid information loss entirely. The explicit ordering of IT2 → IT3 → IT4 is a legitimate DSR design trajectory: start with the simplest data-level fix, then move to loss-level fixes that preserve all data.

***

### 3. RUS vs. alternatives for NLP text classification
**Issue:** Recent NLP surveys recommend **algorithm-level methods** (class-weighted loss, focal loss) over data-level resampling for text classification, precisely because oversampling/undersampling disrupts the natural language distribution. The EACL 2023 survey  specifically notes that for deep NLP models, loss-function modifications tend to outperform sampling methods. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12217700/)

**Your defense:** This is not a flaw in IT2 — it is exactly *why* IT3 and IT4 exist in your design. You should explicitly state in your thesis that IT2 serves as a **data-level baseline** for imbalance mitigation, and that its expected limitations (information loss, potential underfitting) motivate the algorithm-level approaches in IT3 and IT4. Cite the EACL 2023 survey  to frame this narrative. [aclanthology](https://aclanthology.org/2023.eacl-main.38.pdf)
